PBAC & ReBAC

Choosing the Right Authorization Model for Modern Enterprise Security

As organizations face increasingly complex authorization challenges with distributed architectures, cloud services, and diverse user bases, traditional access control methods are showing their limitations. Policy-Based Access Control (PBAC) and Relationship-Based Access Control (ReBAC) represent two modern approaches to solving these challenges, each with distinct advantages for different use cases.

Relationship-Based Access Control

ReBAC determines access based on the relationships between entities in a system. Pioneered by Google's Zanzibar, it models permissions as a graph where nodes represent entities (users, resources) and edges represent relationships, enabling complex hierarchical and social permissions.

Real-World Applications

Healthcare Systems

PBAC excels in healthcare where access depends on multiple factors: practitioner role, patient consent, location (in-hospital vs remote), time of access, and data sensitivity. Policies like "Doctors can access patient records during business hours from hospital networks" are easily expressed.

Social Networks

ReBAC naturally models social relationships: friends, followers, blocked users. Access policies like "view posts from friends and friends-of-friends" or "share photos with specific groups" map directly to the relationship graph.

Financial Services

Banks use PBAC to enforce complex compliance rules: transaction limits based on user verification level, geographical restrictions, time-based controls, and risk scores. Natural language policies simplify audit and compliance.

Document Management

ReBAC handles folder hierarchies naturally: owners of folders automatically get permissions on contained files. Team memberships, project relationships, and organizational structures flow through the permission graph.

Key Differences Explained

Authorization Philosophy

PBAC: "What conditions must be true for access?" - Evaluates multiple factors (attributes, context, environment) against defined policies.

ReBAC: "What relationships exist between entities?" - Traverses relationship graphs to determine access paths.

Policy Management

PBAC: Centralized policy engine with business-friendly UI. Non-technical users can create and manage policies using natural language.

ReBAC: Relationship data stored in graph database. Requires understanding of entity relationships and graph structures.

Evolution from Traditional Models

PBAC: Builds upon RBAC and ABAC, solving role explosion while maintaining familiar concepts. Can incorporate existing roles and attributes.

ReBAC: Represents a paradigm shift from role-based thinking to relationship-based thinking. More dramatic change but powerful for certain use cases.

Making the Right Choice

Both PBAC and ReBAC are powerful modern authorization models. Your choice depends on your specific requirements, existing infrastructure, and use cases.

Choose PBAC When You Have:

  • Complex compliance requirements (HIPAA, GDPR, SOX)
  • Need for business-user-friendly policy management
  • Multiple contextual factors affecting access (time, location, risk)
  • Existing RBAC/ABAC systems to modernize
  • Dynamic authorization requirements

Choose ReBAC When You Have:

  • Natural hierarchical or graph-like data structures
  • Social or collaborative features
  • Complex organizational relationships
  • Need to model "friends of friends" type permissions
  • Document/file system with inherited permissions
